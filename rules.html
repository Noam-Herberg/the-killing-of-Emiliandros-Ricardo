At The Table

Most checks are simple.

Roll percentile dice. If the result is equal to or below the relevant characteristic after modifiers, you succeed. Every full 10 points of difference is a degree of success or failure.

Skill Tests

  • Pick the most relevant characteristic or trained skill.
  • Easy +30, Routine +20, Ordinary +10, Challenging +0, Difficult -10, Hard -20, Very Hard -30.
  • Roll d100. Equal to or below the target succeeds.
  • Each full 10 by which you pass or fail is one degree. Example: target 45, roll 23 = 2 degrees of success.

Movement

  • Half Move: move up to your Half Move value and still take another Half Action.
  • Full Move: spend your whole turn moving up to your Full Move value.
  • Charge: Full Action, move up to Charge distance and make one melee attack at +20 WS.
  • Run: Full Action, move up to Run distance. Ranged attacks against you suffer -20; melee attacks against you gain +20 until your next turn.

Actions

  • On your turn you usually get either two Half Actions or one Full Action.
  • Half Actions: Half Aim, Half Move, Standard Attack, Ready, Stand, many reloads.
  • Full Actions: Full Aim, Full Move, Run, Charge, All Out Attack, Suppressing Fire, some reloads.
  • Free Actions: short speech, dropping an item, some talents and powers.
  • Reactions: usually one per round, commonly Dodge or Parry after being hit.

Shooting

  • Roll Ballistic Skill, modified by range, cover, aiming, lighting, and firing mode.
  • Point Blank +30, Short Range +10, Long Range -10, Extreme Range -30.
  • Half Aim +10. Full Aim +20. Called Shot -20.
  • Standard Attack +10. Semi-Auto Burst +0 and can add extra hits with degrees of success. Full Auto Burst -10 and can add more extra hits.
  • Target in cover, darkness, smoke, melee, or bad weather usually means a penalty or blocked shot.

Melee

  • Roll Weapon Skill to hit in close combat.
  • Targets may be able to Dodge or Parry if they have a reaction available.
  • Standard Attack +10. Charge +20. All Out Attack +30, but you cannot Dodge or Parry until your next turn.
  • Defensive Stance gives enemies -20 to hit you in melee but limits your aggression.
  • Ganging up helps: outnumbering a target in melee usually gives attackers a bonus.

Damage

  • Roll weapon damage, add bonuses listed on the weapon, then subtract Toughness Bonus and armour.
  • Penetration reduces the armour that applies. Example: AP 4 hit by Pen 2 counts as AP 2.
  • Damage after reduction comes off Wounds.
  • If Wounds hit 0, further damage causes Critical Damage based on damage type and hit location.
  • Righteous Fury: if a player rolls a natural 10 on damage, check for extra effect at the GM's call.

Cover & Armour

  • Cover has armour points of its own. If the shot passes through cover, subtract cover AP before damage reaches the target.
  • Armour protects specific body locations.
  • Hit location: reverse the attack roll digits. 01-10 Head, 11-20 Right Arm, 21-30 Left Arm, 31-70 Body, 71-85 Right Leg, 86-00 Left Leg.
  • If a target is partly hidden, only exposed locations can be hit unless the attack punches through cover.

Common Qualities

  • Reliable: jams less often. Unreliable: jams more often.
  • Accurate: rewards aiming and good shots, often with extra damage at range.
  • Scatter: stronger at very close range, weaker at longer range.
  • Shocking: target may be stunned if they fail Toughness.
  • Tearing: roll an extra damage die and keep the better result.
  • Toxic: target may suffer poison after being wounded.

Fate & Fear

  • Fate can usually be spent for rerolls, recovery, or a small push when it matters.
  • Fear tests are Willpower tests when facing horror, warp phenomena, or terror.
  • Insanity and Corruption track mental and spiritual damage over time.
  • When uncertain, make a fast ruling and keep the scene moving.
