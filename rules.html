At The Table

Most checks are simple.

Roll percentile dice. If the result is equal to or below the relevant characteristic after modifiers, you succeed. Every full 10 points of difference is a degree of success or failure.

Skill Tests

  • Pick the most relevant characteristic or trained skill.
  • Apply situational modifiers before rolling.
  • Roll d100. Equal to or below the target succeeds.
  • Each full 10 by which you pass or fail is one degree.

Movement

  • Half Move: move up to your Half Move value and still take another Half Action.
  • Full Move: spend your whole turn moving up to your Full Move value.
  • Charge: move in a straight enough line and make one melee attack.
  • Run: move far, but you become easier to hit until your next turn.

Actions

  • On your turn you usually get either two Half Actions or one Full Action.
  • Common Half Actions: Aim, Move, Standard Attack, Ready, Reload, Stand.
  • Common Full Actions: Full Move, Run, Charge, All Out Attack, some reloads.
  • Reactions, such as Dodge or Parry, happen outside your turn when triggered.

Shooting

  • Roll Ballistic Skill, modified by range, cover, aiming, lighting, and firing mode.
  • Short range is easier; long range is harder.
  • Half Aim gives a small bonus; Full Aim gives a larger bonus.
  • Semi-auto and full-auto can score extra hits with enough degrees of success.

Melee

  • Roll Weapon Skill to hit in close combat.
  • Targets may be able to Dodge or Parry if they have a reaction available.
  • Charge improves your attack and gets you into contact.
  • All Out Attack hits harder but prevents defensive reactions.

Damage

  • Roll weapon damage, add bonuses listed on the weapon, then subtract Toughness Bonus and armour.
  • Penetration reduces the armour that applies.
  • Damage after reduction comes off Wounds.
  • When Wounds run out, further damage causes critical effects.

Cover & Armour

  • Cover can make you harder to hit or absorb damage, depending on the situation.
  • Armour protects specific body locations.
  • If hit location matters, reverse the attack roll digits to find the location.
  • Primitive, Shocking, Toxic, Tearing, and other qualities change how attacks resolve.

Fate & Fear

  • Fate can usually be spent for rerolls, recovery, or a small push when it matters.
  • Fear tests are Willpower tests when facing horror, warp phenomena, or terror.
  • Insanity and Corruption track mental and spiritual damage over time.
  • When uncertain, make a fast ruling and keep the scene moving.
