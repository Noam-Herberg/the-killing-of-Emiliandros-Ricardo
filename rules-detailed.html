Combat Turn Options

Aim
Half Action for +10 or Full Action for +20 to your next attack. Taking a Reaction before attacking loses the bonus.
Standard Attack
Half Action, one melee or ranged attack at +10 WS/BS.
Semi-Auto Burst
Half Action ranged attack at +0 BS. Extra hit for every 2 degrees of success, limited by weapon RoF.
Full Auto Burst
Half Action ranged attack at -10 BS. Extra hit for every degree of success, limited by weapon RoF.
All Out Attack
Full Action melee attack at +30 WS. You cannot Dodge or Parry until your next turn.
Charge
Full Action move into melee and attack at +20 WS. Final movement should be direct.
Suppressing Fire
Full Action. Fire into an arc at -20 BS; targets test Pinning or become Pinned even if no damage is dealt.
Overwatch
Full Action. Set a kill zone and trigger ranged attacks when declared conditions are met.

Defensive & Utility Actions

Evasion
Reaction after being hit. Dodge works against melee, ranged, and hazards; Parry works against melee.
Guarded Action
Half Action. Take -10 to WS/BS, gain +10 to Evasion until your next turn.
Defensive Stance
Full Action. Gain an extra Reaction and enemies suffer -20 WS to hit you in melee.
Disengage
Full Action. Leave melee without provoking a free attack.
Ready
Half Action. Draw, stow, or prepare an item. Dropping an item is normally Free.
Reload
Action length depends on the weapon. Reloads can stretch across turns if listed that way.

Common Attack Modifiers

Point Blank
Within 2m: +30 BS unless engaged in melee.
Short Range
Less than half listed range: +10 BS.
Long Range
More than double listed range: -10 BS.
Extreme Range
More than triple listed range: -30 BS.
Called Shot
Specific hit location: -20 WS/BS.
Shooting Into Melee
Usually -20 BS unless the target is helpless, stunned, or unaware.
Darkness
Usually -20 WS and -30 BS; Stealth often improves.
Fog, Smoke, Mist
Usually -20 BS; concealed targets may gain Stealth bonuses.
Prone Target
Melee attacks gain +10. Ranged attacks suffer -10.
Unaware Target
Attacks usually gain +30.
Outnumbering
2:1 in melee gives +10 WS; 3:1 or more gives +20 WS.
Higher Ground
Usually +10 WS in melee.

Pinning, Cover & Terrain

  • Pinning is normally a Challenging (+0) Willpower test, often caused by suppressing fire or terrifying incoming fire.
  • Pinned characters only take a Half Action, suffer -20 BS, and must seek or remain in cover unless retreating.
  • A Pinned character can test Willpower at the end of their turn to recover; being in cover or no longer under fire can make this easier.
  • Cover protects only covered locations and has its own AP. Excess damage can degrade or punch through cover at GM discretion.
  • Difficult terrain commonly penalizes movement, melee, or Evasion by -10 to -30 depending on severity.

Hit Locations & Multiple Hits

  • For a normal attack, reverse the attack roll digits to find the hit location.
  • 01-10 Head, 11-20 Right Arm, 21-30 Left Arm, 31-70 Body, 71-85 Right Leg, 86-00 Left Leg.
  • Multiple hits usually walk across adjacent locations based on the first hit location.
  • Area attacks, random damage, or environmental hazards may roll a fresh d100 for location.

Conditions

Stunned
No actions or reactions. Attackers gain +20 WS/BS, but the target is not automatically helpless.
Blood Loss
At the start of each turn, suffer Fatigue. A character can attempt Medicae at -10 to staunch it.
Fatigue
If Fatigue exceeds Toughness Bonus + Willpower Bonus, the character falls unconscious. Rest removes Fatigue over time.
Fire
Burning characters suffer Energy damage and Fatigue each round, and must test Willpower to act normally.
Blinded
Automatically fails vision-based tests; severe penalties to WS and many other actions.
Unconscious
No actions, unaware, and treated as helpless.

Using Psychic Powers

  1. Choose a power the psyker knows and check its Action, Focus Power test, Range, Sustained entry, Subtype, and Effect.
  2. Choose the Psy Rating being used, up to the psyker's rating. Many ranges, damage values, and bonuses scale from this number.
  3. Spend the listed action. Most powers are Half Action or Full Action, but some are Reactions, Free Actions, Special actions, or Extended Actions.
  4. Roll the listed Focus Power test. This is usually Willpower or Psyniscience, sometimes opposed by the target.
  5. If the Focus Power test succeeds, apply the power's effect. Use degrees of success when the power says they matter.
  6. If the Focus Power roll triggers Psychic Phenomena, resolve that after the test result. The power can still succeed unless the result says otherwise.

Power Labels

Focus Power
The test used to manifest the power. If it is opposed, compare degrees of success as normal.
PR
Psy Rating used for the power. PR often sets range, radius, number of targets, damage, or bonuses.
Sustained
If a power can be sustained, the entry lists the action needed each turn to keep it active. If the psyker stops sustaining it, the effect ends.
Attack
Counts as an Attack action. A character normally cannot take more than one Attack action in a turn.
Concentration
Requires mental focus. A character normally cannot take more than one Concentration action in a turn.
Opposed
The target rolls the listed test against the psyker's Focus Power test. The winner resists or applies the effect according to the power.

Psychic Attack Types

Psychic Bolt
Works like a single-shot ranged attack. Use the Focus Power roll to hit and reverse the roll for hit location.
Psychic Barrage
Works like semi-auto fire. Extra hits come from degrees of success, but total hits cannot exceed the Psy Rating used.
Psychic Storm
Works like full-auto fire. Extra hits come faster than Barrage, but total hits still cannot exceed the Psy Rating used.
Psychic Blast
Works like a Blast weapon. The radius is based on Psy Rating, and anyone in the area can be affected.
Psychic Melee
If a power creates or enhances a melee attack, resolve the attack as the power states. Some use WS, some use Focus Power.

Psychic Phenomena & Perils

  • When a Focus Power roll triggers Psychic Phenomena, roll on the Psychic Phenomena table and apply the result.
  • If the phenomena result instructs a Perils of the Warp roll, roll on the Perils table instead. Perils are the severe warp backlash results.
  • Phenomena can be atmospheric, frightening, dangerous, or corrupting. Perils can injure the psyker, harm bystanders, summon warp effects, or cause major corruption.
  • The power's success and the warp backlash are separate: a power can manifest successfully and still produce dangerous phenomena.
  • Sanctioned psykers are legal but not safe. Witnesses, priests, enforcers, and Mechanicus personnel may react badly to obvious warp events.
  • In this campaign, any visible Perils result is also a narrative alarm: it may attract cult attention, daemonic interest, or official suspicion.

Maintaining & Interrupting Powers

  • Check the Sustained column. A power with "No" ends after its immediate effect; a power with an action cost can be kept running.
  • Sustaining a power costs the listed action each turn, often Free Action or Half Action.
  • If the psyker is Stunned, Unconscious, killed, or unable to take the sustaining action, the sustained power usually ends.
  • Moving out of range often ends the benefit for allies or targets, depending on the power wording.
  • Some powers forbid voluntary ending or have special failure clauses. Follow the individual power text when it overrides the general procedure.
